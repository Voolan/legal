## Voolan Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: May 10, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy (the “AUP”) explains what is not allowed when using Voolan, including the Voolan Telegram bot, VPN subscription access, websites, support channels, subscription links, credentials, and related services (collectively, the “Service”).

This AUP is part of the Voolan Terms of Service. By using the Service, you agree to this AUP.

### 1. General rule

You must use the Service lawfully, responsibly, and only for personal purposes allowed by your plan.

You must not use the Service to harm Voolan, other users, Telegram, payment providers, infrastructure providers, third-party networks, websites, services, devices, or any person.

This AUP is not exhaustive. We may take action against harmful, abusive, fraudulent, unlawful, or high-risk activity even if it is not specifically listed below.

You are responsible for violations committed through your Telegram account, subscription link, VPN credentials, devices, or access, including violations by anyone who uses your access with or without your permission.

### 2. Territory availability controls

The Service is not available for use in prohibited countries or regions. Current prohibited countries and regions: Russia.

You must not use the Service while located in a prohibited country or region. You must not attempt to bypass, manipulate, or evade territory-availability controls.

Prohibited conduct includes:

- using another VPN, proxy, relay, remote desktop, hosting server, shared account, or similar method to access or purchase the Service from a prohibited country or region;
- manipulating network headers, location signals, device signals, account signals, payment signals, or bot flows;
- asking another person to purchase, activate, renew, or operate the Service for you while you are located in a prohibited country or region;
- using a subscription link or credentials that were issued to someone else to avoid territory controls;
- repeatedly attempting to connect after access was suspended for a territory-of-use signal;
- providing false information during manual review.

If prohibited territory use is detected or reasonably suspected, access may be suspended, subscription links may be disabled or rotated, VPN credentials may be deprovisioned, and purchases or reissues may be blocked.

### 3. Personal use, sharing, and resale

Unless your plan expressly says otherwise, the Service is for personal use only.

You must not:

- share, publish, sell, rent, lease, sublicense, transfer, or resell your subscription link or VPN credentials;
- post credentials in public or private groups, channels, websites, forums, marketplaces, paste sites, repositories, bots, or files;
- operate shared public access, public proxy access, public VPN access, paid access, managed access, or team access through a personal plan;
- use your subscription for another person’s traffic, business, bot farm, scraping infrastructure, spam operation, or automation service;
- bypass or attempt to bypass device/session limits;
- use compromised, leaked, purchased, borrowed, or stolen credentials.

If sharing, resale, compromise, or unauthorized use is detected or reasonably suspected, we may disable old credentials, rotate the subscription link, suspend access, refuse reissue, or terminate the subscription.

### 4. Illegal activity

You must not use the Service to violate applicable law or help anyone else violate applicable law.

Prohibited activity includes, but is not limited to:

- fraud, scams, deception, identity theft, account theft, financial crime, or money laundering;
- phishing, pharming, spoofing, impersonation, credential theft, or fake login pages;
- malware, ransomware, spyware, botnets, command-and-control infrastructure, exploit kits, droppers, loaders, or malicious scripts;
- unauthorized access to systems, accounts, networks, data, or devices;
- trafficking, sale, or distribution of illegal goods or services;
- child sexual abuse material, sexual exploitation, grooming, or any content or activity involving harm to minors;
- threats, stalking, harassment, extortion, doxxing, blackmail, or non-consensual intimate content;
- terrorism support, violent extremism, or credible threats of violence;
- unlawful gambling, regulated financial activity, or controlled-substance activity where prohibited;
- distribution of content that has been found unlawful by a competent authority.

### 5. Security violations and network abuse

You must not use the Service to attack, disrupt, overload, scan, probe, exploit, or gain unauthorized access to any system.

Prohibited activity includes:

- DDoS attacks, stress testing, booting, flooding, amplification, reflection, or traffic generation attacks;
- port scanning, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, exploit testing, or enumeration of third-party systems without authorization;
- credential stuffing, password spraying, brute forcing, token guessing, account checking, or automated login abuse;
- exploitation of vulnerabilities, backdoors, misconfigurations, exposed admin panels, or leaked credentials;
- interfering with routers, DNS servers, mail servers, APIs, cloud systems, websites, games, messaging services, or public networks;
- bypassing rate limits, access controls, CAPTCHAs, anti-bot systems, fraud systems, or abuse-prevention systems;
- operating malware, botnet nodes, scanning nodes, spam nodes, or command-and-control endpoints;
- attempting to attack, scan, reverse engineer, bypass, overload, or interfere with Voolan infrastructure.

Security research is not allowed through the Service unless you have clear authorization from the target and your activity does not create risk for Voolan, our providers, or third parties.

### 6. Spam, messaging abuse, and platform abuse

You must not use the Service for spam or abusive messaging.

Prohibited activity includes:

- unsolicited bulk email, spam, or email bombing;
- SMS spam, messaging-app spam, social-network spam, comment spam, forum spam, or review spam;
- Telegram spam, fake engagement, mass joining, mass inviting, mass messaging, or bot abuse;
- creating, verifying, warming, selling, or operating fake accounts or account farms;
- evading platform bans, rate limits, abuse controls, or anti-spam systems;
- sending messages that are deceptive, malicious, unlawful, or designed to harvest credentials.

### 7. Fraud, abuse of payments, and account manipulation

You must not use the Service to commit payment abuse or account manipulation.

Prohibited activity includes:

- chargeback abuse, refund abuse, trial abuse, coupon abuse, or subscription-cycle manipulation;
- using stolen, compromised, or unauthorized payment methods;
- buying access for a person located in a prohibited country or region;
- creating multiple Telegram accounts to bypass limits, blocks, bans, territory controls, payment controls, or abuse controls;
- selling access to accounts, payment flows, subscription links, or credentials;
- making false claims to support or payment providers.

### 8. Intellectual property and content abuse

You must not use the Service to infringe or help infringe intellectual property, privacy, publicity, or other rights.

Prohibited activity includes:

- unauthorized distribution of copyrighted content;
- operating piracy, counterfeit, cracking, warez, stolen-account, stolen-key, or similar services;
- bypassing technical protection measures;
- distributing leaked private data, stolen databases, credential lists, or non-consensual private material;
- impersonating brands, services, or individuals;
- using the Service to host, seed, promote, or coordinate unlawful infringement.

### 9. Resource abuse and unfair use

You must not use the Service in a way that harms stability, degrades service for others, or creates disproportionate operational risk.

Prohibited activity includes:

- excessive automated traffic, scraping, crawling, or API use that causes complaints or risk;
- bandwidth patterns consistent with abuse, attacks, spam, bot traffic, or resale;
- running open proxies, public relays, public VPNs, exit nodes, tunnels, or gateways for third parties;
- using the Service for datacenter-like workloads, hosting, continuous automated jobs, bot farms, or traffic resale;
- intentionally exhausting IP reputation, port capacity, server resources, or provider limits;
- repeatedly reconnecting, rotating, or scripting access to bypass controls;
- interfering with other users’ performance or our providers’ acceptable use rules.

We may apply technical limits, rate limits, server changes, credential rotation, suspension, or termination to protect the Service.

### 10. Circumvention of Voolan controls

You must not bypass, disable, manipulate, or interfere with Voolan controls.

This includes controls related to:

- territory availability;
- device/session limits;
- subscription expiry;
- payment status;
- credential rotation;
- abuse detection;
- bot onboarding;
- support verification;
- suspension, blocking, or deprovisioning;
- server routing, configuration, or access restrictions.

Attempting to bypass controls may result in immediate suspension or termination without refund, except where a refund is required by applicable law.

### 11. Compromised access

If you suspect that your Telegram account, subscription link, VPN credentials, device, or configuration file has been compromised, you must contact support promptly.

We may rotate credentials, disable old links, deprovision keys, require manual review, or suspend access while investigating.

If compromise resulted from sharing, resale, publication, negligence, or violation of this AUP, reissue may be refused and refunds may be unavailable.

### 12. Abuse reports and investigations

We may investigate suspected violations based on internal signals, user reports, infrastructure-provider complaints, abuse-desk notices, payment-provider notices, platform reports, third-party complaints, or legal requests.

During an investigation, we may:

- suspend or limit access;
- disable or rotate credentials;
- block traffic or destinations;
- preserve relevant logs or audit records;
- request additional information from you;
- contact providers, platforms, abuse desks, or competent authorities;
- refuse new purchases, renewals, or reissues.

We do not guarantee that we will investigate every report or provide details about detection methods, complainants, infrastructure, or other users.

### 13. Enforcement

If we determine or reasonably suspect that you violated this AUP, we may take one or more actions, with or without prior notice:

- warning;
- temporary suspension;
- permanent termination;
- device/session limit reduction;
- traffic blocking or rate limiting;
- server or location restriction;
- subscription link rotation;
- VPN credential deprovisioning;
- refusal of support, reissue, renewal, or refund;
- blocking future purchases;
- reporting to infrastructure providers, platforms, payment providers, abuse desks, regulators, or competent authorities where appropriate.

We may take action even if the violation was unintentional or committed by someone else using your account, link, device, or credentials.

Suspension or termination for AUP violations does not entitle you to a refund, except where required by applicable law.

### 14. Repeat violations

Repeated violations, repeated compromise signals, repeated territory-of-use violations, repeated abuse complaints, or attempts to evade enforcement may result in permanent loss of access and refusal of future service.

A one-time reissue, if offered, does not create a right to future reissues. If you believe that a repeated territory-of-use signal, suspension, credential action, or reissue refusal is incorrect, you may contact support through the Voolan Telegram bot support flow and request manual review. Manual review does not guarantee reinstatement, reissue, refund, or access from a prohibited country or region.

voolan@tutamail.com

Include relevant timestamps, source/destination IPs if available, logs, screenshots, headers, complaint details, and a clear description of the issue. Do not include unnecessary personal data.

We may update this AUP from time to time. The updated version becomes effective when posted in the bot, on the website, or through another reasonable notice method, unless a later effective date is stated.

Your continued use of the Service after the effective date means you accept the updated AUP.
