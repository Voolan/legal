## Voolan Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Voolan collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information when you use the Voolan Telegram bot, VPN subscription access, websites, support channels, subscription links, credentials, and related services (collectively, the “Service”).

### 1. Who controls your data

“Voolan”, “we”, “us”, and “our” mean the operator of the Voolan Service.

Controller / operator legal name: Voolan

Contact for privacy requests: voolan@tutamail.com

Telegram, app stores, payment processors, hosting providers, DNS providers, and other third parties may separately process your data under their own privacy policies.

### 2. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to data processed by Voolan in connection with the Service.

It does not apply to:

- Telegram’s own apps, accounts, payments, Stars, or platform services;
- app stores, payment processors, banks, wallet providers, or mobile carriers;
- VPN client apps that are not operated by Voolan;
- websites, apps, or services you access while connected to the VPN;
- third-party infrastructure providers acting under their own terms.

### 3. Key privacy principles

We aim to process only the data needed to provide, secure, support, and protect the Service.

We design audit and security logs to store signal keys, status values, reasons, timestamps, and sanitized summaries where possible. Audit logs should not contain raw subscription tokens, VPN private keys, passwords, full payment credentials, card details, or unnecessary personal information.

Operational systems may need to store or process limited credentials, identifiers, or configuration data required to provide the Service. Access to operational secrets should be limited and protected.

We do not intentionally log the content of your VPN traffic, the websites you visit, the apps you use through the VPN, the messages you send, or the DNS queries you make, unless explicitly required for a specific troubleshooting or abuse investigation and only to the extent legally and technically appropriate.

### 4. Data we collect

The exact data collected depends on how you use the Service.

### 4.1 Telegram account and bot data

We may collect and process:

- Telegram user ID;
- Telegram username, display name, and profile language if available;
- bot commands, button clicks, onboarding status, and consent confirmations;
- support messages sent through the bot or support channel;
- account state, such as active, expired, suspended, blocked, under review, or deleted;
- timestamps for bot interactions and subscription actions.

### 4.2 Subscription and payment-status data

We may collect and process:

- subscription plan, duration, price, currency or Stars amount shown in the purchase flow;
- subscription start date, end date, renewal state, expiry state, and delivery state;
- payment status, delivery status, refund status, dispute status, or failure status;
- Telegram Stars payment event data and transaction references made available to the bot;
- internal order IDs, invoice IDs, receipt references, and audit records;
- support records related to payment or delivery issues.

We do not receive or store your card number, app-store payment credentials, bank login, or full payment account credentials when you pay through Telegram Stars or other third-party payment flows.

### 4.3 VPN access and operational data

We may collect and process:

- subscription link status, such as active, rotated, disabled, expired, or compromised;
- credential state, such as provisioned, rotated, deprovisioned, suspended, or revoked;
- server or location assigned to your subscription;
- device/session count or connection-limit status;
- connection timestamps needed for limits, troubleshooting, security, and abuse prevention;
- traffic counters or bandwidth totals where needed for fair use, server health, or abuse detection;
- error codes, delivery status, and service health events;
- server-side security events and abuse indicators.

Where possible, we avoid storing raw secrets in audit logs. For example, audit logs should record that a subscription link was rotated or a credential was deprovisioned, not the raw token or private credential itself.

### 4.4 Territory, security, fraud-prevention, and compliance signals

To enforce Service availability, prevent fraud, protect infrastructure, and comply with Service Rules, we may collect and process compliance and security signals, including:

- country or region signal;
- trusted geo/IP signal;
- IP-derived country or region;
- trusted proxy, CDN, or infrastructure geolocation headers;
- network, ASN, hosting-provider, proxy, datacenter, or VPN indicators;
- language, locale, timezone, or bot-interface hints;
- account state, subscription state, payment state, and delivery state;
- repeated mismatch or risk patterns;
- audit events, decision reasons, manual-review notes, and sanitized summaries.

These signals are used to assess territory of use and risk. They are not used to make decisions based on who you are, your citizenship, nationality, ethnicity, language, place of birth, or similar identity attributes.

Territory signals may be inaccurate. If your access is suspended due to a suspected territory signal and you believe this is incorrect, you may contact support for manual review. During review, access may remain suspended.

### 4.5 Support and communications data

If you contact support, we may process:

- your Telegram ID, username, and display name;
- your messages and attachments;
- screenshots or diagnostics you choose to send;
- support notes, status, and resolution history;
- abuse reports or complaints involving your subscription.

You should not send passwords, raw subscription tokens, private keys, payment-card data, identity documents, or sensitive personal data unless we specifically request them and explain why.

### 4.6 Website and technical data

If we operate a website, documentation, status page, or hosted policy pages, we may collect:

- IP address or partial IP address;
- browser, device, operating system, and user-agent information;
- timestamps, referrer, pages visited, and error logs;
- cookie or local-storage data if the website uses them;
- security logs such as firewall, rate-limit, or bot-protection events.

No third-party analytics are currently used.

### 5. Data we do not intentionally collect

We do not intentionally collect or store:

- the content of your VPN traffic;
- the websites, pages, or content you access through the VPN;
- your private messages sent through third-party apps;
- your passwords for third-party services;
- your full card number or bank login credentials;
- raw payment credentials processed by Telegram, app stores, banks, or payment processors;
- raw subscription tokens, VPN UUIDs, private keys, or passwords inside audit logs;
- special categories of personal data unless you voluntarily send them to support or a legal obligation requires processing.

This section does not prevent temporary processing that may occur technically in transit to provide the VPN connection, nor does it prevent limited processing required for security, abuse investigation, legal compliance, debugging, or manual support where strictly necessary.

### 6. Why we use data

We process data for the following purposes:

- to create, maintain, and identify your Service account;
- to deliver subscriptions, subscription links, credentials, and access;
- to process payment-status events, refunds, delivery issues, and subscription expiry;
- to provide customer support and troubleshoot technical problems;
- to enforce plan limits, device/session limits, and Service Rules;
- to detect compromised, shared, leaked, or resold access links;
- to enforce territory-of-use availability restrictions;
- to prevent fraud, spam, abuse, DDoS, malware, phishing, unauthorized access, and network attacks;
- to protect users, Voolan, infrastructure providers, Telegram, payment providers, and third parties;
- to comply with applicable law, legal process, provider obligations, platform rules, and audit requirements;
- to improve reliability, capacity planning, server health, and service quality;
- to communicate important Service changes, security notices, and policy updates.

### 7. Legal bases where privacy law requires them

Where applicable privacy law requires a legal basis, we rely on one or more of the following:

- Performance of a contract: to provide subscriptions, access, support, billing-status handling, account management, and requested Service features.
- Legitimate interests: to secure the Service, prevent fraud and abuse, enforce device limits, enforce territory-of-use restrictions, investigate misuse, protect infrastructure, improve reliability, and defend legal claims.
- Legal obligation: to comply with applicable law, valid legal requests, tax or accounting obligations, sanctions or compliance requirements, and provider or platform obligations where legally required.
- Consent: where we specifically ask for consent for optional processing, such as optional diagnostics or certain cookies, if used.

Where we rely on legitimate interests, we consider the impact on users and use data minimization, access limits, retention limits, and security safeguards where practical.

### 8. Territory-of-use controls

The Service is not available for use in prohibited countries or regions. Current prohibited countries and regions: Russia. We may process territory-of-use signals to determine whether access should be allowed, suspended, reviewed, disabled, rotated, or deprovisioned.

This processing concerns only the territory from which the Service is used. It is not intended to identify or restrict users based on who they are, their citizenship, nationality, ethnicity, language, place of birth, or similar identity attributes.

If a paid subscription is suspended because of a territory signal and there is a risk that the subscription link or credentials were shared or compromised, we may record the suspension reason and may provide a discretionary one-time reissue. A reissue may involve disabling old credentials, invalidating the old subscription link, creating new credentials, and recording that a reissue was used.

### 9. Sharing of data

We may share data with the following categories of recipients when necessary:

- Telegram, where the Service is provided through Telegram bot features, Telegram Stars, payment events, support flows, or platform functionality;
- hosting, VPS, network, anti-DDoS, DNS, domain, CDN, monitoring, backup, and infrastructure providers;
- payment processors, app stores, or platform providers where needed to process payment status, refunds, disputes, tax, or fraud issues;
- support, ticketing, email, or communication providers if we use them;
- professional advisers, auditors, accountants, or legal advisers;
- law enforcement, regulators, courts, infrastructure providers, or abuse desks where required by law or where necessary to investigate or respond to serious abuse, security incidents, or legal claims;
- another operator if the Service is merged, transferred, reorganized, or sold, subject to reasonable safeguards.

We do not sell personal data to advertisers. We do not allow third parties to use VPN operational logs for their own advertising profiles.

### 10. International transfers

Because the Service uses Telegram, hosting providers, network providers, and other internet infrastructure, data may be processed in countries other than your country of residence or the country where you use the Service.

Where applicable law requires transfer safeguards, we will use appropriate safeguards where practical, such as contractual protections, provider due diligence, data minimization, encryption, access controls, and retention limits.

### 11. Retention

We keep data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer period is required by law, dispute, abuse investigation, tax/accounting obligation, provider requirement, or legal claim.

Our standard retention periods are:

| Data category | Retention period |
|---|---|
| Telegram account and subscription state | While the account or subscription is active, then up to 12 months after expiry or last interaction |
| Payment-status, invoice, refund, and accounting records | As long as reasonably needed for accounting, tax, fraud-prevention, dispute-handling, and legal-compliance purposes |
| Security, territory, fraud, and access-control audit logs | Usually up to 30 days, unless a longer period is needed for abuse investigation, dispute handling, fraud prevention, legal compliance, or infrastructure security |
| VPN operational connection metadata needed for limits or security | As short as practical, usually from a few hours up to 30 days depending on implementation and security needs |
| Support messages | Usually up to 90 days after the last support interaction, unless a longer period is needed for disputes, abuse history, account recovery, or legal compliance |
| Backups | Until overwritten under the backup rotation schedule |
| Abuse reports and enforcement records | As long as reasonably needed to prevent repeated abuse, handle provider complaints, respond to legal requests, or defend claims |

### 12. Security

We use technical and organizational measures designed to protect data against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, and disclosure. These measures may include access controls, credential rotation, separation of audit logs from secrets, restricted admin access, encryption where practical, infrastructure hardening, backup controls, monitoring, and abuse-response procedures.

No system is completely secure. You are responsible for securing your Telegram account, devices, VPN client apps, operating system, and local network.

### 13. Your choices and rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights to:

- request access to personal data we hold about you;
- request correction of inaccurate data;
- request deletion of data;
- object to or restrict certain processing;
- request portability of certain data;
- withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
- lodge a complaint with a data protection authority.

These rights may be limited where we need to keep data for legal, security, fraud-prevention, abuse-prevention, accounting, dispute, or infrastructure-protection reasons.

To make a request, contact us using the contact method in this Policy. We may need to verify your Telegram account or request limited information to confirm that you are the legitimate account holder. Do not send passwords, raw subscription links, private keys, or sensitive documents unless we specifically request them.

### 14. Automated decisions and manual review

Some access-control decisions may be assisted by automated technical signals, such as territory signal, payment status, account state, abuse score, device/session limit, or credential compromise signal.

Where a decision significantly affects paid access and applicable law requires human review, you may contact support to request manual review. Manual review does not guarantee reinstatement, reissue, refund, or access in a prohibited country or region.

### 15. Children

The Service is not intended for children. You must be old enough to use Telegram, make digital purchases, and enter into a binding agreement under the laws applicable to you.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we learn that a child has used the Service without appropriate legal basis or consent, we may delete the account data and terminate access.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version becomes effective when posted in the bot, on the website, or through another reasonable notice method, unless a later effective date is stated.

If changes materially affect how we process personal data, we will try to provide reasonable notice where practical.
